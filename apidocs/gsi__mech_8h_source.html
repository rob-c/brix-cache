4/*
5 * gsi_mech.h — server-side Globus GSI GSSAPI mechanism over a mem-BIO OpenSSL
6 * TLS handshake (phase-82 §1.9 / §2.8). Drives the RFC 2228 AUTH GSSAPI / ADAT
7 * token exchange that gsiftp clients (globus-url-copy, gfal2) speak:
8 *
9 * 1. a TLS 1.2 handshake carried in base64 ADAT tokens (335 continue / 235
10 * done), the client presenting an RFC-3820 proxy as its certificate;
11 * 2. the GSI credential-delegation sub-exchange layered over that channel as
12 * application-data: delegator sends 'D', we (acceptor) reply with a proxy
13 * certificate REQUEST, the delegator signs it, we assemble the delegated
14 * credential (brix_gsi_build_pxyreq / brix_gsi_assemble_proxy);
15 * 3. post-auth confidentiality: control commands/replies are GSS-wrapped
16 * (SSL_write/SSL_read) as RFC 2228 MIC/CONF/ENC tokens.
17 *
18 * The engine is transport-agnostic: the caller base64-decodes each ADAT/MIC/ENC
19 * argument and feeds the raw token in; the out token is raw bytes to base64 back
20 * onto the wire. TLS is pinned to 1.2 because the GSI ADAT state machine expects
21 * the acceptor's final flight (ChangeCipherSpec+Finished) in the last token — the
22 * TLS 1.3 flight shape breaks the 335/235 semantics.
23 */
