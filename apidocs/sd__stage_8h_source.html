4/*
5 * sd_stage.h - the generic write-stage decorator (phase-64 section 12.2; was the
6 * phase-63 C-2/C-6 write-back stager).
7 *
8 * WHAT: An SD-driver instance that WRAPS a source backend (`source`) and an
9 * explicit STAGE STORE instance (`store`, any driver), interposing only the
10 * staged-write path: a staged upload lands on the stage store and is FLUSHED
11 * to the backend on commit through the one staging engine. Every read /
12 * namespace / xattr op delegates straight to `source` (open returns the
13 * source's own object, so reads bypass the decorator).
14 *
15 * WHY: Phase-63 hard-coded the stage area as a local POSIX directory under the
16 * export root and promoted with a bespoke temp-file loop. Phase-64 makes the
17 * stage area a first-class TIER (`store`, which may be local or a remote
18 * stage server) and routes the flush through brix_stage_submit / _run_inline
19 * - the ONE async-staging engine every write-back/upload/migrate path shares
20 * (P5, G9). A posix stage store is byte-equivalent to phase-63.
21 *
22 * HOW: staged_open -> store->driver->staged_open; staged_write ->
23 * store->driver->staged_write; staged_commit publishes the object on the
24 * stage store then brix_stage_run_inline(FLUSH, store, key, source, key)
25 * moves it to the backend (sync) - the durable async-defer arrives with the
26 * SP4 queue; staged_abort drops the stage temp. See docs/refactor/phase-64-
27 * fully-tiered-composable-storage.md (section 11, 12.2, Appendix J4).
28 */
