1/*
2 * scan_emit.h — ordered-emit reorder buffer for the storage-scan engine.
3 *
4 * WHAT: a small, pure, allocation-free reorder window. Worker threads finish
5 * per-object actions out of order but each carries a monotonic sequence
6 * number (assigned by the single producer walk); this buffer releases
7 * records to the emit callback strictly in sequence order.
8 * WHY: emitting in walk order is what makes the `after=<cursor>` resume
9 * contract sound — a cursor P means "every path ≤ P in walk order is
10 * done." Isolating the reorder logic keeps it unit-testable (no threads,
11 * no nginx) — the engine wraps it under its emit mutex.
12 * HOW: caller-owned storage (slots[window]/used[window]); submit(seq,payload)
13 * buffers then drains every now-contiguous record via the callback.
14 */
