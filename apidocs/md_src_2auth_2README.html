auth — identity and authorization

Everything that establishes who a client is (GSI/x509, WLCG tokens, SSS, Kerberos, password, unix, host) and what they may do (the authz gate: authdb + VO ACLs + token scope), plus the crypto plumbing those mechanisms share and the impersonation broker that performs storage operations as the mapped user.

Identity establishment runs during the protocol handshake; authorization runs per-operation through the auth gate (authz/) — the sole checkpoint on cached-serve paths.

Dir What
authz/ path-level authorization: auth gate, authdb, VO ACLs, verdict caches
gsi/ GSI/x509: proxy certs (RFC 3820), delegation, DH, signing policy, CRL
token/ WLCG/SciTokens JWT validation, JWKS, scopes, issuer registry
sss/ Simple Shared Secret credentials (cluster-internal auth)
krb5/ Kerberos 5 authentication
pwd/ password authentication
unix/ unix identity mapping
host/ host-based trust
voms/ VOMS attribute-certificate extraction (VO/groups/roles)
crypto/ shared crypto helpers: OCSP, cert stores, policy
impersonate/ per-user impersonation broker (setfsuid workers, idmap)

Invariant: S3 SigV4 is NOT here — it is request signing, not identity, and lives in src/protocols/s3/ (CLAUDE.md INVARIANT 6: never share auth logic between SigV4 and WLCG tokens).
