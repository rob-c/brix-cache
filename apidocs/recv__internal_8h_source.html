4/*
5 * cms/recv_internal.h — private cross-file contract for the CMS client-side
6 * (manager-connection) receive path.
7 *
8 * WHAT: Declares the two entry points shared between the three translation
9 * units that together implement the receive path of our connection UP to a CMS
10 * manager — the forwarded-namespace-op executor (recv_forward.c), the opcode
11 * frame handlers + dispatch table (recv_frame.c), and the read event loop
12 * (recv.c). ngx_brix_cms_process_frame() is the frame router called by the
13 * read loop; cms_node_exec_forward() is the Plane-B mutation executor called by
14 * the forwarded-op frame handler.
15 *
16 * WHY: recv.c was a single 828-line file spanning three distinct concerns
17 * (Phase-79 file-size split): the forwarded-namespace-op storage machinery, the
18 * opcode handlers + redirect/wake plumbing, and the read/framing event loop.
19 * Splitting it into focused units keeps each file under the 500-line guideline
20 * and lets each concern be reviewed in isolation. The only cost is that two
21 * functions that were `static` now cross a translation-unit boundary; those —
22 * and only those — are declared here so the definer and the caller agree on the
23 * prototype (a header-declared symbol MUST be non-static at its definition or
24 * the link fails).
25 *
26 * HOW: Include after "cms_internal.h" (which provides ngx_brix_cms_ctx_t) and
27 * "node_ops.h" (which provides brix_cms_node_plan_t). Purely internal — never
28 * included outside this module.
29 *
30 * Requires: cms_internal.h before inclusion.
31 */
