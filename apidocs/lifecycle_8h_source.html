4/*
5 * lifecycle.h — nginx integration glue for per-request UNIX impersonation
6 * (phase 40).
7 *
8 * WHAT: The configuration directives, config-time validation, master-side broker
9 * spawn, worker-side client connect, and per-request principal set/clear that
10 * bridge the self-contained impersonation engine (idmap/broker/client) into the
11 * nginx module lifecycle.
12 *
13 * WHY: Concentrating ALL nginx-typed glue here keeps the load-bearing module
14 * files (module.c, process.c, postconfiguration.c) touched by only a single
15 * call each, and keeps the engine itself free of ngx_conf/ngx_cycle types.
16 *
17 * HOW: One process-global settings block (there is at most one broker per nginx
18 * instance) populated by the `brix_impersonation*` directives; init_module
19 * spawns the broker (FRM double-fork) only in `map` mode on a root master;
20 * init_process connects the worker client; the request hooks set the broker's
21 * target principal from the authenticated identity for the duration of one
22 * synchronous op-dispatch. Off-by-default: in `off`/`single` mode nothing here
23 * spawns a process, opens a socket, or routes any I/O through the broker.
24 */
