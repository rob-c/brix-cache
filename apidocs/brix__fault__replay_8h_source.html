1/*
2 * brix_fault_replay.h — session record / replay for brix-fault-proxy.
3 *
4 * RECORD: append every byte segment the proxy forwards (in either direction,
5 * after all faults have been applied — i.e. exactly what the peers saw) to a
6 * capture file, each framed with its direction and a millisecond timestamp
7 * relative to proxy start. This gives a deterministic, byte-exact transcript of
8 * a real session.
9 *
10 * REPLAY: load a capture back into memory so the proxy can act as a synthetic
11 * peer — feeding a client the recorded upstream (or client) byte timeline with
12 * the original inter-segment timing, no live upstream needed. Turns a
13 * once-seen, hard-to-reproduce server behaviour (a malformed response, an exact
14 * partial-write pattern, a timing-dependent bug) into a repeatable fixture.
15 *
16 * File format: 5-byte magic "BFPR\1", then records of
17 * [dir:1 ('u'=client->upstream, 'd'=upstream->client)]
18 * [ts_ms:8 big-endian][len:4 big-endian][len payload bytes].
19 *
20 * Recording is process-global and mutex-guarded (many relay threads). The
21 * replay store is caller-owned and immutable once loaded.
22 */
