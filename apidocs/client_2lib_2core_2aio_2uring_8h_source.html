4/* ---- File: client/lib/uring.h — optional io_uring disk ring (Phase 44) ----
5 *
6 * WHAT: A thin liburing wrapper for the native client. CB-W2 uses it to give
7 * the xrdcp transfer pump disk⇄network overlap: a deep queue of buffers
8 * lets the local disk write of chunk k proceed while the network read of
9 * chunk k+1 is in flight (download), and symmetrically for uploads — while
10 * still presenting the synchronous, in-order, one-chunk face the pump
11 * expects (Option A). The socket event loop engine swap (CB-W4) layers on
12 * top later.
13 *
14 * WHY: The pump (client/lib/copy.c) is strictly serial today — a download
15 * blocks the network read until the disk write completes. Overlapping
16 * them is the single biggest local-disk streaming win, and it is fully
17 * isolated behind two adapters, so the pump's tested cancel/progress/
18 * short-read discipline is untouched.
19 *
20 * HOW: Everything is gated by BRIX_HAVE_LIBURING. When undefined the whole
21 * TU compiles to inert stubs (brix_uring_available() -> 0, create -> NULL),
22 * so the epoll/pread path is the only thing in the binary and there is no
23 * -luring dependency — identical to the always-compiled krb5 stub model. */

50/*
