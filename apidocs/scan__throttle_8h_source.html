1/*
2 * scan_throttle.h — pure rate/budget math for the storage-scan engine.
3 *
4 * WHAT: the leaky/token-bucket arithmetic, the budget check, and the adaptive
5 * multiplier — all pure and deterministic over an explicit `now_ns`, so
6 * they unit-test in isolation (scan_unittest.c) with synthetic clocks.
7 * WHY: the engine's primary CEPH protection is a byte-rate cap on backend
8 * reads; bounding the *math* separately from the mutex/condvar wrapper
9 * keeps the policy testable and the concurrency layer thin.
10 * HOW: a token bucket accrues `rate_bps` tokens/s up to `capacity`; a worker
11 * about to read `need` bytes asks how long to wait. Adaptive shrinks the
12 * effective rate under foreground pressure / rising backend latency.
13 */
