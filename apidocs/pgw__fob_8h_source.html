1/*
2 * src/write/pgw_fob.h — kXR_pgwrite CSE uncorrected-page registry (the "Fob").
3 *
4 * WHAT: A fixed-size set (BRIX_PGW_FOB_SLOTS entries) embedded in
5 * brix_file_t recording the pages that failed CRC32c on a kXR_pgwrite
6 * and have not yet been corrected by a kXR_pgRetry resend. Mirrors stock
7 * XrdXrootdPgwFob: an entry is keyed by (offset << kXR_pgPageBL) |
8 * (dlen < kXR_pgPageSZ ? dlen : 0) so partial (unaligned) last pages are
9 * distinct from full pages at the same offset.
10 *
11 * WHY: Under the accept-then-correct (CSE) protocol the server writes corrupt
12 * bytes to disk and reports them to the client, which must resend each
13 * page. kXR_close fails with kXR_ChkSumErr while any page remains in the
14 * Fob — that close gate is what guarantees a committed file never holds
15 * known-corrupt data.
16 *
17 * HOW: Linear O(N) scan over at most BRIX_PGW_FOB_SLOTS (256) entries; no
18 * heap. All callers run on the single connection event-loop thread, so
19 * no locking is needed (unlike stock's mutex-guarded std::set).
20 */
