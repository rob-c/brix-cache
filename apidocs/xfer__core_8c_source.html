1/*
2 * xfer_core.c — the transfer engine's terminal chokepoint.
3 *
4 * WHAT: brix_xfer_finish() — the single call every transfer kind makes when it
5 * reaches a terminal state (commit or abort). It records the outcome
6 * through the unified ledger.
7 *
8 * WHY: Before this, each kind (STAGE in vfs_staged.c, TAPE in frm/stage.c, WT in
9 * writethrough_flush.c) built an brix_xfer_audit_t inline and called the
10 * ledger — the same six-field boilerplate copied at seven sites. Funnelling
11 * every terminal transition through one function removes that duplication
12 * (one fewer place to get the audit wrong) and, crucially, gives the
13 * durable journal a single hook to update completion state in a later step
14 * without revisiting any caller. This is the BEGIN/MOVE/COMMIT/ABORT
15 * envelope's COMMIT/ABORT edge; the rest of the envelope lands with the
16 * journal generalization (spec §9 Phase 4).
17 *
18 * HOW: Pure composition over the ledger — no new state, side effects at the edge.
19 * No goto.
20 */
