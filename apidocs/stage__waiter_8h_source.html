1/*
2 * stage_waiter.h — async stage/recall completion waiter table.
3 *
4 * WHAT: A small SHM table of clients parked on an in-flight recall. When a
5 * kXR_open hits a nearline file and async recall is on, the request is
6 * acknowledged with kXR_waitresp and a waiter row is recorded
7 * {reqid, streamid, conn_fd, conn_number, worker_pid}. When the recall
8 * completes, the worker that staged it marks every matching waiter ready; each
9 * owning worker then delivers the real open response via kXR_attn(asynresp) on
10 * the parked streamid — no poll/retry, full disconnect tolerance.
11 *
12 * WHY: The FRM-dissolution (§13b, phase-64 P6) re-home of the FRM waiter
13 * (former src/frm/waiter.c). The park/wake substrate is protocol-layer (only the
14 * owning worker may write to a parked connection), so it lives beside the stage
15 * engine + request registry rather than in a backend driver. The recall itself
16 * is driven by the sd_frm backend / stage engine; this table only tracks who is
17 * parked and delivers the in-place open response on completion.
18 *
19 * Cross-worker design (no IPC): the staging worker is usually NOT the worker that
20 * parked the waiter. brix_stage_waiter_deliver() marks all matching rows ready
21 * under the SHM lock; each worker only ever touches its OWN connections,
22 * delivering its ready rows from brix_stage_waiter_poll_local() (called on the
23 * stage scheduler tick). This keeps every wire write on the owning worker.
24 *
25 * See docs/superpowers/plans/2026-07-01-frm-dissolution.md (Task 2).
26 */
