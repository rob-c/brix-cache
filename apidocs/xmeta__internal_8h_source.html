5 * WHAT: Cross-declares the block-geometry helpers (defined in xmeta.c) and the
6 * two persisted wire descriptors (the STATE section struct and the ORIGIN
7 * fixed-header size) that the encode and decode halves both need.
8 * WHY: xmeta.c (942 lines) split into three focused files under the 500-line
9 * cap: xmeta.c (record lifecycle, bitmap ops, DIGEST-list ops),
10 * xmeta_encode.c (record → wire buffer) and xmeta_decode.c (wire buffer →
11 * record). The bitmap byte-length / block-count math is used by all three;
12 * xmeta_state_wire_t and XMETA_ORIGIN_FIXED describe on-disk layout and
13 * must be identical on the encode and decode sides — so exactly those
14 * symbols become non-static / shared here, nothing else crosses.
15 * HOW: xmeta.c, xmeta_encode.c and xmeta_decode.c all include this header; none
16 * of the symbols is exported beyond the xmeta codec unit.
17 *
18 * Requires: xmeta.h (brix_xmeta_t) — included below.
19 */
