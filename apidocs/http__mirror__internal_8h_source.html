1/*
2 * http_mirror_internal.h — declarations shared across the HTTP/WebDAV traffic
3 * mirror files after the phase-79 file-size split.
4 *
5 * WHAT: Cross-declares the one method-classification predicate and the five
6 * upstream callbacks that straddle the http_mirror.c / http_mirror_request.c
7 * boundary, plus the shared MIR_HTTP_INC counter macro.
8 * WHY: http_mirror.c (949 lines) split into three focused files under the
9 * 500-line cap: http_mirror.c (method classifiers + phase-handler /
10 * subrequest orchestration), http_mirror_request.c (shadow HTTP
11 * request/response build + nginx upstream callbacks), and
12 * http_mirror_config.c (merge-time upstream setup + directive setters).
13 * The subrequest driver in http_mirror.c wires the five upstream callbacks
14 * that live in http_mirror_request.c, and the request builder there reuses
15 * the body-method predicate that lives in http_mirror.c — exactly those
16 * symbols become non-static and are declared here; nothing else crosses.
17 * HOW: http_mirror.c and http_mirror_request.c both include this header; none
18 * of the symbols is part of the mirror's public surface (http_mirror.h).
19 *
20 * Requires: protocols/webdav/webdav.h (ngx_http_request_t and the brix metrics
21 * accessor used by MIR_HTTP_INC) included before this header —
22 * satisfied transitively via http_mirror.h.
23 */
