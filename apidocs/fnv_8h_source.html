1/* ---------------------------------------------------------------------------
2 * fnv.h — single source of truth for the FNV-1a hash constants.
3 *
4 * WHAT: The two magic values that define each FNV-1a variant — the offset
5 * basis (seed) and the prime (per-byte multiplier) — in both the 64-bit
6 * and 32-bit widths the tree uses.
7 *
8 * WHY: These constants were duplicated as bare literals across five hashers
9 * (config fingerprint, cvmfs geo/gate routing, SHM kv, per-VO metrics,
10 * negcache), some in hex and some in decimal, so a reader could not tell
11 * at a glance that two sites hash identically. One named home keeps the
12 * algorithm's identity obvious and the values impossible to mistype.
13 *
14 * HOW: Compile-time integer macros only — a hasher seeds `h` with the offset
15 * basis, then `h = (h ^ byte) * PRIME` per byte (FNV-1a byte order).
16 * These are the canonical published FNV-1a constants; do not "tune" them.
17 * ------------------------------------------------------------------------- */
18
