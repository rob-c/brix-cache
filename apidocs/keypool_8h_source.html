4/*
5 * Phase 33 — per-worker pool of pre-generated ephemeral ffdhe2048 DH keys.
6 *
7 * WHAT: The GSI round-1 (kXGC_certreq) response needs a fresh ephemeral DH key.
8 * This module hands them out from a per-worker warm pool instead of
9 * generating one inline.
10 *
11 * WHY: Generating the key inline on the single nginx event thread
12 * head-of-line-blocks EVERY other connection on the worker for the duration
13 * of the keygen. Under a concurrent GSI-handshake burst (grid pilots, FTS,
14 * xrdcp --sources) that serializes N keygens on one thread and stalls all
15 * other I/O — the exact concurrency wedge this fixes.
16 *
17 * HOW: A warm batch is generated synchronously at worker start (no connections
18 * yet). The pool is refilled by a thread-pool task: the keygen runs on a
19 * worker thread into task-local storage, and only the nginx event thread
20 * mutates the pool (pop on certreq, push from the refill done-callback) —
21 * so no locking is required. If the pool is momentarily empty the certreq
22 * handler falls back to an inline keygen (correct, just not offloaded).
23 *
24 * The ffdhe2048 key is standalone (RFC 7919 fixed group; no per-connection
25 * inputs), so pre-generating it ahead of time is sound and preserves perfect
26 * forward secrecy (each key is handed out to exactly one session, then freed).
27 */
