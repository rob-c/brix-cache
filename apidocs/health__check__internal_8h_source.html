5 * WHAT: Cross-declares the per-probe context struct, its phase enum, the one
6 * metric-increment macro, and the four functions that straddle the
7 * health_check.c / health_check_probe.c boundary.
8 * WHY: health_check.c (934 lines) split into two focused files under the
9 * 600-line cap: health_check.c (connection lifecycle + manager entry —
10 * ctx_create/resolve/open_conn/queue_bootstrap/begin_connect/start/timer/
11 * manager_start plus the shared brix_hc_finish termination point) and
12 * health_check_probe.c (the async probe I/O state machine —
13 * flush/send_probe/write+read handlers/recv_frame/TLS handshake/dispatch).
14 * The lifecycle side wires the probe's read/write handlers onto the
15 * connection and flushes the pipelined bootstrap; the probe side reports
16 * every verdict through brix_hc_finish — exactly the struct, the phase
17 * enum, the metric macro, and those four functions cross the boundary.
18 * HOW: both files include this header; none of these symbols is part of the
19 * health-check public surface (health_check.h). brix_hc_flush,
20 * brix_hc_read_handler and brix_hc_write_handler are defined non-static in
21 * health_check_probe.c; brix_hc_finish is defined non-static in
22 * health_check.c.
23 *
24 * Requires: health_check.h (→ core/ngx_brix_module.h, which transitively
25 * provides XRD_RESPONSE_HDR_LEN and the wire types) and

33/*

35 * Each phase consumes the server's reply to the request the previous phase (or
36 * the pipelined bootstrap) sent: HANDSHAKE -> PROTOCOL -> LOGIN -> PROBE. See
37 * brix_hc_dispatch() for the per-phase transitions and early-exit verdicts.
