1/*
2 * stage_request_registry_internal.h — private on-disk layout + substrate seam
3 * shared across the three halves of the durable stage-request registry after the
4 * phase-79 file-size split.
5 *
6 * WHAT: Declares the private on-disk format (constants, header/record structs,
7 * the registry handle, the lock-mode enum) and the substrate entry points
8 * — durable file I/O, whole-file locking, slot scan/alloc, and reqid /
9 * status / view translation — that the mutating and read op files call
10 * across the file boundary.
11 * WHY: stage_request_registry.c was one 956-line file owning three concerns:
12 * the durable-store substrate + lifecycle, the mutating client ops, and
13 * the read/query ops. Splitting the two op groups into sibling files keeps
14 * each translation unit under the 500-line cap and focused; the substrate
15 * they both build on stays in stage_request_registry.c and is reached only
16 * through the entry points declared here. The exact SHM-free fcntl+mutex
17 * lock/unlock boundaries and the fixed-slot scan are unchanged — the split
18 * is purely a re-home, not a behavior change.
19 * HOW: stage_request_registry.c defines every symbol declared here (the former
20 * static substrate helpers, now extern); stage_request_registry_mutate.c
21 * and stage_request_registry_query.c include this header and call them.
22 * None of these symbols is exported beyond the stage-request module — the
23 * public contract stays in stage_request_registry.h.
24 *
25 * Requires: stage_request_registry.h (public types) before inclusion.
26 */
