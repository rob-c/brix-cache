1/*
2 * async_job.c — minimal async job lifecycle helpers.
3 *
4 * See async_job.h for the public API.
5 *
6 * WHAT: Three thin functions that manage the finalized flag and dispatch the
7 * cleanup callback exactly once.
8 * WHY: Double-free and double-finalize bugs in background job teardown paths
9 * (TPC threads, PUT body callbacks, Qckscan) share the same root cause:
10 * cleanup logic is not idempotent. Centralising the once-run guard
11 * prevents diverging fixes across protocol surfaces.
12 * HOW: brix_async_job_cleanup_once() checks and sets job->finalized under
13 * no lock — callers must ensure the function is not called concurrently.
14 * The nginx model (main-thread completion callbacks) naturally provides
15 * this guarantee for HTTP and stream request paths.
16 */
