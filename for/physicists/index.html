Your tools already work with it.

You don't run the server, and you shouldn't have to care how it's built. Point xrdcp, rucio, aws-cli, or a browser at it and copy your data — the same way you already do.

Why it helps your day

Fewer surprises between you and your data.

just-works

No new client, no new habits

BriX-Cache speaks the protocols your tools already use: xrdcp, xrdfs, the Python XRootD client, rucio, the AWS CLI, boto3, and ordinary browsers. Same URLs, same commands.

one-tree

One file, reachable three ways

The same POSIX tree is visible at once over root://, davs://, and S3. Checksums, metadata, and extended attributes match no matter which protocol you use — copy with xrdcp, verify with aws s3, get the same bytes.

resilient

Transfers finish on a bad network

Flaky WiFi or a lossy transatlantic link doesn't mean a failed copy. The native client rides out packet loss — reconnect, re-auth, reopen, and resume at the byte offset — over root:// and over HTTP via Range requests.

honest

Never fast at the cost of correct

A transfer that completes is always byte-exact. Under heavy loss it slows down — it does not corrupt or silently truncate. Integrity is never traded for speed.
